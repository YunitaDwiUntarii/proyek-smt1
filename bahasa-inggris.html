Ice Cream Stick Waste

Waste is leftover material or residue produced from goods or production processes. Waste itself has several types: organic, inorganic, and hazardous (B3) waste. Its physical forms consist of solid waste, liquid waste, gas waste, and even sound waste. Ways to manage waste include the 3R principles: Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle.

Waste is leftover material or residue produced from goods or production processes. Waste itself has several types: organic, inorganic, and hazardous (B3) waste. Its physical forms consist of solid waste, liquid waste, gas waste, and even sound waste. Ways to manage waste include the 3R principles: Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle.

Ice cream stick waste is waste produced from ice cream. The types of ice cream sticks are made of wood and plastic, but most commonly used are wooden ice cream sticks. Ice cream stick waste has a long and thin shape. The function of ice cream sticks is to serve as a handle for the ice cream.

To obtain ice cream stick waste, we can find it in many places. Most people who eat ice cream immediately throw away the sticks. Some people sell ice cream sticks separately at relatively low prices—only around Rp 3,000 and you can get quite a lot of sticks. Efforts to reduce ice cream stick waste can be made by turning them into various crafts.

The functions of ice cream stick waste include being made into furniture and various handicrafts. Flower vases, pencil boxes, and night lamps can be craft options. By continuously using recycled waste, we help conserve natural resources. We can even help many people by creating job opportunities through waste processing.
